## 4x4 Full Matrix Results (16,000 games, eval_final/)

Paired average winrates (P0+P1 averaged, 2,000 games per pair):

| P0 \ P1 | Random | Greedy | Aggressive | FlatMC |
|---------|--------|--------|------------|--------|
| Random  | 50.0%  | 17.6%  | 27.0%      | 18.4%  |
| Greedy  | 82.4%  | 50.0%  | 90.2%      | 65.6%  |
| Aggressive | 73.0% | 9.8%   | 50.0%      | 10.0%  |
| FlatMC  | 81.6%  | 65.0%  | 90.0%      | 50.0%  |

Key observations:
- Aggressive has a severe P0 bias in mirror (86.6% P0 vs 13.4% P1) -- tiebreak bug
- Greedy mirror also has P0 bias (63.7%) but less extreme
- FlatMC mirrors Greedy almost exactly -- delegates to Greedy rollouts, adds no value
- Aggressive loses badly to Greedy/FlatMC (P0 9-10%) -- rush strategy fails vs construction-heavy opponents

## Key Findings

1. **Tiebreak bug fixed**: P0 first-move advantage was artifact of tiebreak always preferring P0. After fix, paired P0 calibration drops from ~85% to ~50% in most cases. Random mirror now 48-52%.

2. **Game has tactical depth but limited strategic depth**: Aggressive vs Greedy shows meaningful tactical decisions (rush vs develop), but all games converge to the same construction-victory endgame under Greedy variants. The 13-tech tree has only 1 viable path (C-tree).

3. **FlatMC adds zero value**: Flat MC rollouts delegate to Greedy for simulation, so FlatMC behavior is indistinguishable from Greedy. 5 sims vs 100 sims makes no difference. Rollout policy must be at least Random to provide signal.

4. **Greedy v1 > v4 (over-engineering hurts)**: Adding economic optimization, balanced strategies, and more complex heuristics consistently degrades performance. Simpler is better.

5. **Evo plateau at gen5**: Evolutionary parameter tuning hits 89.5% vs Greedy at generation 5, then slowly rises to 93.5% by gen 200. Diminishing returns suggest manual tuning would achieve similar results.

6. **BC is a specialist, not a generalist**: 100% construction wins vs Greedy (9-turn games, perfect build order cloning), but 0% vs Random (cannot respond to combat). Overfitting to one opponent.

7. **DQN results unverified**: Claims of 92% vs Random on a 6-action discrete action space. No evidence of generalization to original per-unit action space. Training log contains placeholders (TBD).

8. **Randomness helps underdogs**: Deterministic combat makes Greedy stronger by 4.2% vs Random. Random combat (3d6) benefits the weaker player.

## Known Issues

- **Aggressive mirror tiebreak**: Aggressive vs Aggressive has 86.6% P0 winrate due to deterministic first-move advantage in tiebreak. Minor issue (mirror only) but should be documented.
- **BC generalization failure**: BC has 0% winrate vs Random -- cannot generalize beyond training distribution (greedy opponents).
- **DQN unverified**: DQN evaluation results are incomplete. Marked as "TBD" in the paradigms report. Do not cite DQN numbers in AI comparisons.
- **Evo evaluation cost**: Each Evo gen evaluation requires ~5 games per individual. Gen 200 x 30 pop x 5 games = 30,000 games. Expensive to reproduce.
- **FlatMC/Greedy identity problem**: FlatMC delegates to Greedy internally, making it impossible to distinguish from Greedy in play style. Misleading to treat as separate AI.
- **No unit-level DQN**: DQN works on 6-action discrete space (strategy choice), not the original per-unit action space. The 92% claim is on a simplified problem.
- **v0.5.0 plan**: See next section for planned improvements.
